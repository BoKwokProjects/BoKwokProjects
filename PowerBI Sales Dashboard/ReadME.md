üìä Business Insights & Recommendations
1. Sales Trends and Seasonality

Insight:
Monthly sales trends reveal consistent seasonal patterns across multiple years, with stronger performance in the second half of the year, particularly during late summer and year-end periods.

Recommendation:
Inventory planning, workforce allocation, and promotional campaigns should be aligned with peak demand periods. Early preparation for high-demand months can reduce stock shortages and maximise revenue potential.

2. Product Category and Sub-Category Performance

Insight:
Sales are concentrated in key product categories, particularly Technology and Office Supplies, with certain sub-categories driving a disproportionate share of revenue.

Recommendation:
High-performing product lines should be prioritised for expansion and innovation. Underperforming sub-categories should be reviewed for potential repricing, promotion, or rationalisation to improve overall portfolio efficiency.

3. Customer Segmentation and Value Distribution

Insight:
Customer segmentation shows that High-Value and Mid-Value customers together account for the majority of total sales, while Low-Value customers contribute a relatively small proportion of revenue.

Recommendation:
Implement targeted retention and loyalty programmes for high-value customers, such as exclusive discounts, priority customer support, early access to new products, and personalised account management.
For mid-value customers, apply personalised marketing and cross-selling strategies, including product recommendations based on purchase history, bundled offers, and targeted email campaigns to encourage higher spending.
For low-value customers, optimise service costs by increasing the use of automated support systems, self-service platforms, and standardised communication channels, while limiting high-cost manual interventions.

4. Top Customer Contribution and Risk Exposure

Insight:
The Top 10 customers generate a significant proportion of total revenue. However, profit levels vary considerably across these customers, with some high-revenue clients delivering relatively low margins.

Recommendation:
Customer-level profitability should be monitored regularly. Pricing structures and discount policies for high-volume, low-margin customers should be reviewed to ensure sustainable long-term relationships and reduced dependency risk.

5. Profitability and Quadrant Analysis

Insight:
The Profit vs Sales scatter plot highlights four distinct customer groups. Several customers fall into the high-sales but low-profit quadrant, indicating margin pressure and potential cost inefficiencies.

Recommendation:
Retention strategies should focus on high-sales, high-profit customers as core revenue drivers. For high-sales, low-profit customers, cost structures and pricing policies should be reassessed. Marketing efforts should target high-profit, low-sales customers to unlock growth potential.
